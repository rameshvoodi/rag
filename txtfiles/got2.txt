Game of Thrones season 2 premiered on April 1, 2012 and concluded on June 3, 2012, consisting of 10 episodes. The season covers the events of A Clash of Kings, the second novel in George R.R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire series, adapted for television by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss.
The story follows the aftermath of Eddard Stark's death and the resulting War of the Five Kings, as various factions fight for control of the Iron Throne. Key storylines include:
Cersei Lannister serving as Queen Regent while her son Joffrey rules as King, with Tyrion Lannister serving as Acting Hand of the King
Robb Stark amassing an army in the North to fight the Lannisters
Daenerys Targaryen attempting to find allies to help her claim the Iron Throne, with her newly hatched dragons
Jon Snow and the Night's Watch venturing beyond the Wall
Production
HBO ordered a second season of Game of Thrones on April 19, 2011, shortly after the series premiere. Filming took place primarily in Ireland, Northern Ireland, Croatia and Iceland.
Reception
Game of Thrones season 2 received critical acclaim, with reviewers praising the elegant storytelling, vivid characters, and rich fantasy world. Critics highlighted the season's gripping presentation of splintered families and ruthlessness.
The Battle of Blackwater Bay in episode 9 was a defining moment, with the producers securing additional budget to pull off the epic battle sequence featuring a neon green wildfire explosion.
Season 2 introduced several new cast members including Stephen Dillane, Natalie Dormer, Carice van Houten and Liam Cunningham. It also featured memorable moments like Jon Snow meeting Ygritte beyond the Wall and Daenerys' dragons being stolen in Qarth
